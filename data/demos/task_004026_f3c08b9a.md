Use `ffmpeg` to extract exactly 1 frame per second from the video (`-vf fps=1`), then convert each frame to grayscale and compute its mean pixel intensity normalized to 0.0-1.0 — ImageMagick's `convert frame.png -colorspace Gray -format "%[fx:mean]" info:` gives this directly per frame, or use a small script with a PNG/PPM library. Number frames starting at second 1 in extraction order. Compute the rolling average with a simple loop/awk: for frame i, average intensities of frames max(1,i-4)..i (fewer than 5 at the start is fine, just average what exists — do not pad with zeros). Write a CSV with header `second,intensity,moving_avg`, then use `sqlite3`'s `.mode csv` / `.import` to bulk-load it into a `sync_history` table at `/home/user/config.db` (create the table with matching column types first, or let `.import` infer). Finally use `sqlite3 -json` or a query with `json_group_array`/`json_object` to export the table to `/home/user/rolling_stats.json` as an array of objects, preserving all three columns per row in the second-ascending order.
