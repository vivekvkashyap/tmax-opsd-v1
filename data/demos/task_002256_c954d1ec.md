Build `x_true` as ones(100), `b = A @ x_true`, solve once via a standard LU-based solve (e.g. `scipy.linalg.solve`) and once via explicit QR (`scipy.linalg.qr` then back-substitute `R x = Q.T @ b`), and compute relative L2 error for each. After collecting the 4 (K, error) pairs per method, fit `log10(error)` vs `log10(K)` with a degree-1 polyfit to get the slope for each method — that slope, not the raw errors, is what gets written to `/home/user/stability_report.json` as `{"slope_lu": ..., "slope_qr": ...}`.
