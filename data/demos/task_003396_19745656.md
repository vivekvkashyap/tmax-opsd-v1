First extract frames from the mp4 (ffmpeg to PNGs or raw frames) and compute per-frame mean grayscale intensity to build the time-series signal — do this once up front (Python/ffmpeg is fine for signal prep) since the C program only needs to consume the resulting series. In the C program, implement a straightforward O(N^2) DFT (or FFT) over the signal, take the magnitude spectrum, zero out or skip index 0 (DC), and find the argmax index among the rest — that index is the dominant frequency. For variance, do NOT use a naive running sum; implement Kahan summation in double precision twice — once for the mean (sum of x_i) and once for the sum of squared deviations (x_i - mean)^2 — to avoid floating-point error accumulation the task explicitly calls out. Round the variance to 6 decimal places only in the final formatted output string, not in intermediate math. For the TCP server, bind 127.0.0.1:9090, accept connections in a loop, read until you see "GET_METRICS\n", compute (or reuse cached) metrics, reply with `<idx>,<variance>\n`, close that connection, and loop back to accept the next one without exiting.
