Treat beep-duration extraction as a signal-processing task, not a manual estimate. Load `/app/telemetry.wav` with `scipy.io.wavfile` or the `wave` module, convert samples to absolute amplitude, and detect beep segments by thresholding amplitude against a small fraction of the signal's max/RMS (silence vs. tone) — find contiguous runs of samples above threshold, convert each run's sample-length to milliseconds using the file's sample rate, and divide by 10 to get the load percentage; round to 1 decimal. Be careful to correctly separate the 30 beeps from the 0.5s silence gaps — a fixed/naive threshold can merge or split beeps if you don't smooth the amplitude envelope first (e.g. a short moving-average or RMS window reduces false triggers from sample-level noise at zero crossings of the sine wave). Verify your extracted beep count is exactly 30 before writing the CSV; if not, tune the threshold/smoothing rather than hardcoding results.
