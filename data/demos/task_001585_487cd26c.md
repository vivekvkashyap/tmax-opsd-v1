Fix it by adding a `sync.Mutex` (or `sync.RWMutex`) guarding every read/write to the map, locking around the read-modify-write of the EMA computation itself, not just the map access, so the update stays atomic. Separately, find the `json.Unmarshal` (or decoder) call and check its error return; replace any `panic(err)` or unchecked ignore with a proper `http.Error(w, ..., http.StatusBadRequest)` and an early `return` so malformed payloads never crash the process.
