Debug the build and the numerics as two separate, verifiable problems. For the build: compile with `gcc ... 2>&1` and read the actual error — a missing `#include <math.h>` causes an implicit-declaration warning/error for `sqrt`, and even after adding the include, linking against libm requires the `-lm` flag on the gcc command in build.sh (link math functions explicitly; don't just silence warnings). For the precision bug: replace the single-pass `sum`/`sum_sq` formula (`sum_sq - sum*sum/n`) — which subtracts two large nearly-equal numbers and can go negative under float rounding — with a genuine two-pass algorithm: first compute the mean over all values, then in a second pass accumulate the sum of squared deviations `(val - mean)^2`, divide by `(n-1)`, and take the square root. Switch every accumulator and intermediate from `float` to `double` throughout, since the low-variance/high-magnitude fuzz inputs will still lose precision in single precision. After compiling, run `fuzz.sh` yourself and inspect `fuzz_results.log` for any NaN before declaring done, rather than assuming the fix worked.
