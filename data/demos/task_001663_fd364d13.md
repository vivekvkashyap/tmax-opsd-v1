Build the numeric pipeline as a sequence of small, independently-checkable transforms, since an off-by-one in the sliding window or a mishandled short/invalid sequence will silently shift every downstream value. Parse the FASTA yourself (split on `>` header lines), and reject any sequence containing a character outside the 20 standard amino-acid codes given in the hydropathy table. Map each valid sequence to its Kyte-Doolittle values using the provided table. For the moving average, only compute the centered window average at positions where a full 9-residue window fits (indices 4..len-5 inclusive of a 0-indexed sequence), and skip sequences too short to yield any window. For the central difference, only use interior points of the smoothed array (drop its first and last element), and skip sequences yielding no derivative values (e.g. `seq4_short` and the invalid one should be excluded naturally by these length/validity checks). Collect the max-abs-derivative per surviving sequence into one array, fit `scipy.stats.gaussian_kde` with default bandwidth, integrate it from 1.0 to 5.0 with `scipy.integrate.quad`, round to 4 decimals for `results.txt`, and also render/save a KDE curve plot over 0..5 to `plot.png` with matplotlib (use a non-interactive backend like `Agg` since there's no display).
