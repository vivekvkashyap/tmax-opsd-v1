Treat this as a "latest-wins" deduplication problem over an append-only log table. In SQLite, use a window function or a correlated subquery to pick, per `(source_id, target_id)` pair, the row with `MAX(updated_at)` — e.g. `GROUP BY source_id, target_id` combined with `updated_at = (SELECT MAX(updated_at) FROM connections_raw r2 WHERE r2.source_id=r1.source_id AND r2.target_id=r1.target_id)`, or a `ROW_NUMBER() OVER (PARTITION BY source_id, target_id ORDER BY updated_at DESC)` filtered to rank 1. Only after deduplication should you filter on `status = 'active'` and join to `servers` to restrict by the source's `env` (the target's env is irrelevant — do not filter on it). Build the adjacency structure with `sqlite3 -json` or piped `jq` grouping: group by source hostname, count targets, and sort each target list alphabetically before emitting `outgoing_count`/`targets`. Common mistakes: filtering `status='active'` before dedup (which keeps stale actives even when a newer inactive superseded them), and forgetting `chmod +x` on the script or that it must take the env name as `$1`.
