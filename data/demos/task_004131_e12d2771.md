Start with dynamic analysis, not decompilation. Run the binary under `strace -f /app/legacy_calc` while piping test integers in, and separately under `gdb --args /app/legacy_calc` with `run` then feeding input, to catch the exact signal (e.g. SIGFPE) and the register state at crash. Binary-search which inputs crash it (try small integers, then check residues mod small primes) to find the crash pattern — it will correlate with a specific modulus. For the non-crashing inputs, collect several (x, f(x)) pairs and fit a small polynomial or check known number-theoretic forms (e.g. quadratic in x) by hand or with a quick script — don't guess blindly. Once you have the formula and the crash condition, build the replacement service with `socat TCP-LISTEN:9999,reuseaddr,fork EXEC:/path/handler.sh` where handler.sh reads a line, computes the formula in `bc` or `awk` (avoiding the division that crashes the original), and echoes the result. Test with `nc` against both crashing and non-crashing inputs before finishing. Make sure the socat process is left running in the background (not attached to your shell) when your turn ends.
