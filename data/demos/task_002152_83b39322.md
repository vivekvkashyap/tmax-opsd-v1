Split `raw_data.csv` into at least 4 chunks (e.g. `split -n l/4` on the data rows, keeping the header handling separate), and process each chunk in a worker (Python/awk) that computes `x`, looks up the matching `id` in `reference.csv` (load it once per worker into a dict/array — don't re-read it row-by-row), and emits anomalous ids to a per-chunk temp file. After all workers finish, concatenate their outputs, dedupe if needed, and `sort -n` before writing the final `anomalies.txt`.
