The core gotcha here is pandas int/float upcasting: after a left-join, `group_id` becomes float64 because of injected NaNs for unmatched users. Fix this explicitly by filling NaNs first — `merged['amount'] = merged['amount'].fillna(0.0)`, `merged['group_id'] = merged['group_id'].fillna(0).astype('int64')` (or nullable `Int64`, but cast to a plain numpy int dtype before doing the bitwise shift, since `<<` on pandas' nullable Int64 or on floats will error/misbehave) — only after that cast will `group_id << 1` work; do this before writing `cleaned_data.csv`. For the modeling half, fit `PCA(n_components=2)` on the three numeric features, `train_test_split(..., test_size=0.2, random_state=42)`, then train `RandomForestClassifier(random_state=42)` and score accuracy on the held-out test set.
