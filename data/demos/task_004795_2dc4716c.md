First get a good initial guess for the frequency: subtract a rough linear trend (or just use the raw signal), compute the FFT (`numpy.fft.rfft` with the correct sample spacing from `t`), and pick the frequency bin with the largest magnitude (excluding the DC/0 Hz bin) as your initial guess for `f`. Estimate initial guesses for the other parameters directly from the data too: `m`/`c` from a linear fit or from the endpoints, `A` from the amplitude range, `alpha` from a rough envelope decay estimate, and `phi` near 0. Then run `scipy.optimize.curve_fit` (or `least_squares`) on the full model `A*exp(-alpha*t)*cos(2*pi*f*t+phi)+m*t+c`, passing your FFT-derived `f` and other guesses as `p0`, and set bounds so `A>0` and `alpha>0` to keep the optimizer numerically stable — without a spectral initial guess for `f`, gradient-based fitting on an oscillatory decay will almost certainly converge to the wrong frequency. Finally dump the six fitted floats to `/home/user/fit_results.json` in the exact required schema.
