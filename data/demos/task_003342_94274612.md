Get the statistics right first, since everything downstream depends on them: compute the mean and *population* standard deviation (divide by N, not N-1) over all `value` entries — mixing this up with sample stddev is the classic mistake here. Classify a row as anomalous when `abs(value - mean) > 2*stddev` (strict inequality), write those rows (with header) to `anomalies.csv` in the original order, and keep the remaining "normal" rows for the regression step. For the trend line, implement OLS manually with the closed-form formulas: `slope = sum((t_i - mean_t)*(v_i - mean_v)) / sum((t_i - mean_t)^2)` and `intercept = mean_v - slope*mean_t`, computed only over the normal (non-anomalous) subset, not the full dataset. Use `float64` throughout in Go to avoid precision drift, and marshal the result to JSON with exactly the keys `slope` and `intercept`.
