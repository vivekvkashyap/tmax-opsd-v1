The core pitfall: a left-merge that introduces NaN silently upcasts an int64 column to float64 — `df.astype()` alone won't help until you fix the underlying float column, and a plain `int` cast will fail on NaN. Use pandas' nullable integer dtype: `df['quality_label'] = df['quality_label'].astype('Int64')` after the merge, so present values stay integers and missing ones become `pd.NA` (which serializes as an empty field in CSV, not `.0` or `NaN`). For the prediction step, split cleanly on `df['quality_label'].isna()` to get train vs. predict rows, fit `TfidfVectorizer()` with default params on the training text (fit_transform on train, transform on the row(s) to predict — don't fit on the full corpus, which would leak information and also isn't what's specified), then fit `LogisticRegression(random_state=42)` on the TF-IDF matrix and labels.
