Treat this as three independent, checkable stages chained by files, not one monolithic script. First, segment audio deterministically: `ffmpeg -i /app/recording.wav -f segment -segment_time 1 -c copy /home/user/segments/out%03d.wav`. Then for each segment run `ffmpeg -i <seg> -af volumedetect -f null /dev/null 2>&1` and grep/parse `max_volume:` and `mean_volume:` from stderr with `sed`/`awk`, stripping the trailing `dB` unit and any sign quirks so the CSV holds bare floats like `-5.4` — skip any segment where `mean_volume` didn't appear (some tail segments produce silence-only or malformed filter output). Second, read `/app/weights.csv` (`intercept,w_max,w_mean`) and compute `prediction = intercept + w_max*max_volume + w_mean*mean_volume` per row with `awk` or `bc -l`, writing `predictions.csv`. Third, implement the bootstrap purely in Bash/awk: for 500 iterations, sample N indices with replacement (e.g. `$RANDOM % N` or `shuf -r -n N`) from the predictions array, compute that sample's mean, accumulate, then divide by 500 for the final grand mean written to `final_result.txt`. Since bootstrap-of-means converges to the plain sample mean, sanity-check your result against a direct average of predictions.csv before finishing.
