For the C++ side, first understand the naive `O(M*N)` approach's exact math: for each of the M grid points, the fraction of N samples less than that value, compared against the truncated-Normal CDF, summed as a Riemann sum. The fast approach is to `std::sort` the N samples once, then for each grid point use `std::upper_bound` (or a two-pointer sweep since both sequences are monotonic) to get the ECDF value in O(log N) or amortized O(1), giving overall O(N log N + M).
