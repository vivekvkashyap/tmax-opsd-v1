Do the toolchain step first: `cd /app/tools && gcc wav2csv.c -o wav2csv` (no extra libs needed), then run `./wav2csv /app/audio/decay_signal.wav /home/user/signal.csv` to get one normalized amplitude sample per line. In Python, load the CSV, take the first 4000 rows, build the time axis as `t = np.arange(4000) / 8000.0` (the stated 8000 Hz sample rate), and fit the damped-sinusoid model `y = A * exp(-gamma * t) * sin(2*pi*f*t)` with `scipy.optimize.curve_fit`, giving it a sensible initial guess — critically, guess `f` from the data itself (e.g. via FFT peak or zero-crossing rate) rather than a fixed arbitrary number, since curve_fit is very sensitive to the initial frequency guess and can converge to the wrong harmonic or fail entirely otherwise; also bound `gamma` and `A` to be positive. Once converged, write `{"A": ..., "gamma": ..., "f": ...}` to `/home/user/params.json`. Sanity-check your fit yourself by reconstructing the waveform from your parameters and plotting/comparing it against the actual first-4000-sample signal before finishing, since the grader compares MSE against the true underlying waveform, not the noisy recording.
