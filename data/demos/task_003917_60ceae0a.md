Compute training statistics only from train.csv, never from test.csv — that is the entire point of the exercise. Use `awk -F, 'NR>1{s+=$2;n++} END{print s/n}' train.csv` to get the mean, then a second awk pass with that mean as a `-v` variable to sum squared deviations and divide by N-1 (sample variance), then `sqrt()` for the sample std dev. Normalize test.csv's feature_A with these fixed train-derived constants (never recompute mean/std on test.csv). To join test.csv with labels.csv on id: strip headers with `tail -n +2`, sort both files with `sort -t, -k1,1`, then use `join -t, -1 1 -2 1`. Finally pipe the joined stream through awk again, passing the train mean/std as `-v` params, computing `z=(x-m)/s`, accumulating `(z-label)^2`, and printing the average with `printf "%.4f\n"` into mse_result.txt. Keep all arithmetic in awk (it handles floats natively) — avoid bc or python. Double-check id sort order matches between test and labels before joining, since `join` requires sorted input on the join key.
