Load `/app/sensor_data.wav` and compute its STFT (e.g. `librosa.stft`), splitting into magnitude (`np.abs`) and phase (`np.angle`) — keep the phase untouched, since only the magnitude gets denoised. Transpose so rows are time frames and columns are frequency bins, build a DataFrame with a `frame_id` column (0..n_frames-1) from the row index, then left-merge with `/app/frame_metadata.csv` on `frame_id`; because that CSV only labels a subset of frames, the merge introduces NaNs in non-STFT metadata columns which will silently upcast frame_id to float unless you explicitly recast — after the merge, force `frame_id` back with `.astype('Int64')` (pandas nullable int) or reconstruct it from the original index rather than trusting the merged column, and don't drop any rows even though some rows have missing metadata. Fit PCA(n_components=2) on the magnitude feature matrix, transform, then `inverse_transform` to get a smoothed/denoised magnitude reconstruction (this is intentionally lossy — that's the "denoising"). Recombine the denoised magnitude with the original phase (`denoised_mag * exp(1j*phase)`) and run `librosa.istft` to get the time-domain signal, then write it out with `scipy.io.wavfile.write` at the original sample rate to `/home/user/recovered_sensor.wav`.
