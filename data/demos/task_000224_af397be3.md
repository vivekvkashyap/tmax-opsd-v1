Approach this as a straightforward compile-run-analyze pipeline; do not try to predict the numeric results yourself. Compile with `gcc -fopenmp /home/user/spectro_sim.c -o /home/user/spectro_sim -lm` — omitting `-fopenmp` or `-lm` will fail to link or silently run single-threaded. Loop 100 times invoking the binary, redirecting each run's stdout to `run_0.txt` .. `run_99.txt` in `/home/user/sim_data/`. In `analyze.py`, for each of the 100 files: load the 1000 values, fit `UnivariateSpline(x, y, s=0.5)`, take its derivative via `.derivative()`, find its roots with `.roots()`, and among those roots pick the one where the spline value is maximal (the global max, not just any critical point — reject minima/saddle roots by comparing spline values). Collect the 100 peak x-locations, compute their mean and variance with numpy, and run `scipy.stats.shapiro` on that array of 100 values. Write all five fields to `/home/user/report.json`, setting `is_reproducible` strictly by checking `variance_peak == 0.0` — do not compute it from a threshold or override it manually, since the underlying atomic-float accumulation is expected to make peaks vary run to run.
