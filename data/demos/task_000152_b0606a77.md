This is a deterministic frame-extraction filter, so match cv2's exact semantics rather than approximating them. Open `/app/profiling.mp4` once with `cv2.VideoCapture`, read the total frame count via `cap.get(cv2.CAP_PROP_FRAME_COUNT)`, and for each stdin line: parse the integer, bounds-check against `[0, total_frames)` and print `-1` for out-of-range or unparseable input, otherwise seek with `cap.set(cv2.CAP_PROP_POS_FRAMES, idx)`, call `cap.read()`, and if it fails treat as `-1`. On success take `frame[0, :, :]` (the top row, all columns, all channels) and print the integer sum of every sub-pixel value — do not average or restrict to one channel. Keep the VideoCapture open across all input lines rather than reopening per line, both for correctness (avoids re-decoding drift) and efficiency. Be careful that seeking with `CAP_PROP_POS_FRAMES` and reading is the standard, reproducible way to get a specific frame; don't try to compute the sum analytically or guess pixel values — actually decode the frame.
