First locate the service: scan ports 8000-9000 on 127.0.0.1 for an open HTTPS listener (e.g. loop with `curl -sk https://127.0.0.1:$p/ -o /dev/null -w '%{http_code}\n'` or `nmap`/`nc`, since it's TLS a plain connect probe plus `openssl s_client` works well). Once found, pull the cert with `openssl s_client -connect 127.0.0.1:<port> </dev/null 2>/dev/null | openssl x509 -noout -subject` to read the CN — that CN is the username you need, don't guess it. For the exploit, build a Rust cargo project using `reqwest` (with `danger_accept_invalid_certs(true)` on the ClientBuilder since the cert is self-signed) and `base64`/`serde_json` to hand-construct a JWT: base64url-encode `{"alg":"none","typ":"JWT"}` and `{"user":"<CN>","role":"admin"}` as the two segments, join with `.`, and per the `alg:none` spec leave the signature segment empty (trailing dot, or no third segment — test both if unsure). Send it as `Authorization: Bearer <token>` to `/flag` and write the exact response body to `/home/user/flag.txt` with no extra whitespace or newline mismatches.
