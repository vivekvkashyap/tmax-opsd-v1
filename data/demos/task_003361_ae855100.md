Parse the custom binary format sequentially with Go's `encoding/binary`, reading a `uint16` little-endian length, then that many raw filename bytes, then a `uint32` little-endian length, then that many content bytes, looping via `io.ReadFull` until EOF (treat EOF at the length-read step as a clean end-of-archive, not an error). The security-critical step is sanitizing each stored filename with `filepath.Base()` before ever constructing an output path — this collapses both absolute paths and `../../` traversal sequences down to just the trailing component, which is exactly what prevents Archive Slip; never concatenate the raw stored name onto the extraction directory. For atomic writes, write content to `safe_extract/<basename>.tmp`, close the file, then `os.Rename` it to `safe_extract/<basename>` (rename is atomic on the same filesystem, unlike a direct write-then-use). While extracting, compute each file's SHA-256 (`crypto/sha256`) over its content, collect `(basename, hexdigest)` pairs, sort them by basename (`sort.Slice` or `sort.Strings` on keys), and write `manifest.txt` lines as `<hex>  <basename>` (two spaces) in that sorted order. Watch for filename collisions after stripping paths — several entries could reduce to the same basename.
