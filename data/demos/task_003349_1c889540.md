Second, parse `payload` per its declared `fmt`: write one regex/parser for JSON (`re.search(r'"id":(\d+).*"category":"(\w)".*"value":([\d.]+)', payload)` or `json.loads`), one for CSV (`payload.split(',')`), one for XML (regex on the tag contents) — don't assume a single format works for all rows. Fourth, detect the changepoint: sort clean deduped records by original stream order (or id), look at `value` over a rolling window, and find where it jumps roughly an order of magnitude and stays elevated — cut everything from that point onward rather than filtering individual outliers. Finally, do a stratified 20% sample per `category` using `random.seed(42)` (or pandas `groupby().sample(frac=0.2, random_state=42)`), then dump the id/category/value triples as JSON.
