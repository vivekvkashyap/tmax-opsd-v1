Design the supervisor as an infinite loop with a short sleep (e.g. 0.2-0.5s) doing three checks each iteration: (1) is the `net_worker` process still alive (check via stored PID and `kill -0` or `ps`)? If not, relaunch it in the background immediately — minimize downtime, since packet count during 20s is the scored metric. (2) What is the size of `/home/user/cache/data.bin`? Since the worker stalls at 8MB but you're told to act at 5MB (5242880 bytes), check proactively and rotate before it ever nears the stall threshold: move the file into `/home/user/backups/` with a unix-timestamp suffix (`$(date +%s)`), gzip it, and let the worker's next write recreate `data.bin` fresh — do this via `mv` then `gzip` on the backup copy, not in place, so you don't lose in-flight writes. (3) is the config backup in place before first launch (`cp /home/user/config.ini /home/user/backups/config.ini.bak` once, at startup, not every loop iteration). Poll frequently enough (sub-second) that you catch both crashes and the size threshold quickly, since the worker writes ~50KB/100ms and the whole budget between 5MB and 8MB is under a second worth of writes at full speed — a slow polling interval will let it stall anyway. Make the script executable.
