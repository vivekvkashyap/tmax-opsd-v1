Get the infrastructure and each processing step working and sanity-checked in isolation before chaining them. Start `/app/start_services.sh` and confirm both Redis (6379) and the Flask service (5000) are actually up, then GET `http://127.0.0.1:5000/data` once to inspect the raw spectra shape (1000x200) and confirm it's noisy/baseline-drifted as described. In process.py, apply `scipy.signal.savgol_filter` along axis=1 (the wavelength axis) with window_length=21, polyorder=3, deriv=2 to strip the polynomial baseline and sharpen peaks — this is the key step that turns a near-singular, drift-dominated matrix into something NMF can actually decompose; then take `np.abs()` since NMF requires non-negative input. Run `sklearn.decomposition.NMF(n_components=3)` on the result, and for each of the 3 resulting component spectra find `np.argmax()` as its peak index. For the confidence intervals, resample rows (with replacement, `np.random.choice` on sample indices) 100 times, rerun the full savgol+NMF+argmax pipeline on each bootstrap sample, collect the 3 peak indices per iteration, sort by mean peak position across bootstraps so component identity stays consistent, then take the 2.5th/97.5th percentiles per position. Sort final peaks by mean_index ascending and write result.json in the exact schema.
