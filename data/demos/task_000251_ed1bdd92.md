(1) In `analyze_audit.sh`, extract frames with ffmpeg to PPM (e.g. `ffmpeg -i /app/vnc_session.mp4 frame_%05d.ppm` in a temp dir) — PPM P6 is trivial to parse: skip the header tokens (magic, width, height, maxval) then read raw binary RGB bytes. (2) Write `detector.cpp` to read consecutive frame pairs in filename order, sum `abs(pixel_a - pixel_b)` over every byte, divide by total subpixel count to get the average difference per transition, and track the index of the maximum — remember frame indices are 0-based and the "difference for frame N" corresponds to the transition between frame N and N+1, so don't off-by-one when writing the winning index. (3) In the shell script, compile with g++, launch the detector in the background with `&`, capture its PID, `wait` on it, then delete the temp PPM directory only after the wait succeeds. (4) Validate incrementally: check a handful of PPM headers parse correctly and print a few sample frame-diff values before trusting the final answer, since a parsing offset error silently shifts every result.
