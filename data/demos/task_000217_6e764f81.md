Inspect `/app/start_services.sh` and `/app/sensor_api/app.py` to see what Redis connection env var the Flask app expects (commonly `REDIS_URL` or similar `os.environ.get(...)` call), start `redis-server` bound to 127.0.0.1:6379, export the matching variable before launching the Flask app, and confirm connectivity with `redis-cli ping` and `curl http://127.0.0.1:8080/data` before writing any fitting code. For the model fit, use `scipy.optimize.curve_fit` with the function `a*exp(b*x) + c*sin(d*x)` — nonlinear fits with an exponential and sinusoid are sensitive to initial guesses, so pick reasonable starting values (e.g. `a` near the data's scale, `b` small and possibly negative for decay, `d` estimated from apparent oscillation frequency in the data via inspection or FFT) rather than defaulting to all-ones, and check the residuals/plot mentally against the returned data to ensure the fit isn't diverging or stuck in a bad local optimum.
