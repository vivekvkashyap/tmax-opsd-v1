Treat `/app/bin/packer` as an untrusted black box you must feed a pre-cleaned directory to — do not try to patch or reverse the binary itself; the fix belongs entirely in your pre-processing. Steps: (1) extract the tar.gz into `/app/staging`; (2) parse `/app/logs/audit.log` by splitting on `BEGIN RECORD`/`END RECORD` blocks (a simple state-machine or regex over multi-line records), collect `Filename:` values from any record whose `Status:` is `VULNERABLE` or `DEPRECATED`, and delete those files from staging; (3) walk `/app/staging` for symlinks using `os.path.islink` (Python) or `find -type l` — for each symlink, resolve its target with `os.path.realpath`/`readlink -f` and reject it (remove the symlink) if the resolved path falls outside `/app/staging` (prefix check) OR if resolution raises/loops (catch loop errors, or detect cycles by following links yourself with a visited-set and a max depth far below the packer's 1000, e.g. abort resolution after ~40 hops if not settled).
