Solve this as a chained-join problem in pure shell/jq, not by writing a general graph library. First filter with `jq -c 'select(.status=="SUCCESS")'` on `logs.jsonl` to get the successful edges, projecting just src/dst/amount. Then compute 2-hop paths by self-joining the filtered edge list on `dst == src` of the next hop: for each edge where `src == "ROOT"`, find all edges whose `src` equals that edge's `dst`, and sum the two amounts — `jq` can do this join directly if you load the full array and use nested `.[]` iteration, or you can pipe through two passes with `join`/`sort` keyed on the intermediate account. For each resulting destination `Y`, take the maximum total amount across all paths reaching it (group by destination, keep max) — in jq this is a `group_by(.dest) | map({dest: .[0].dest, max: (map(.total) | max)})`. Finally sort alphabetically by destination and emit as `Destination,MaxTotalAmount` CSV with a literal header line, writing to `/home/user/data/max_2hop.csv`. Double check destinations reachable only via a FAILED hop are excluded, and that a 2-hop path's total is genuinely the sum of both edge weights, not just the second hop's amount.
