Never call `tar.extractall()` directly on untrusted input — instead iterate `tar.getmembers()` yourself and validate each member before extracting it individually with `tar.extract(member, path=target_dir)`. For path containment, resolve the intended destination as `os.path.realpath(os.path.join(target_dir, member.name))` and check it's equal to or a child of `os.path.realpath(target_dir)` (e.g. via `os.path.commonpath` or a prefix check with a trailing separator to avoid the `/foo` vs `/foobar` false-positive) — this catches both absolute-path members and `../../` traversal in relative names. For symlink/hardlink members, additionally resolve where `member.linkname` would point: if the link is relative, join it against the *directory of the member's own target path* (not the archive root) and apply the same containment check; if `member.linkname` is itself absolute, check it directly. Skip (don't extract) any member that fails either check, and append the exact `member.name` as stored in the tar to the log file, one per line, in iteration order. Handle directories, regular files, symlinks (`TarInfo.issym()`), and hardlinks (`TarInfo.islnk()`) as distinct cases in your validation branch.
