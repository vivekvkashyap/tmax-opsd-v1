Treat this as a full pipeline, not just a curve fit. First load `/app/signal.wav` and compute a spectrogram (e.g. `scipy.signal.stft` or `librosa`) with a short window appropriate for an 8000 Hz, 1 s signal; for each time frame pick the frequency bin with peak magnitude to build a noisy instantaneous-frequency-vs-time trace — this is your observed data, not the answer itself. Fit $f(t)=\alpha e^{\beta t}+\gamma$ to that trace with a nonlinear optimizer (Nelder-Mead/L-BFGS) to get a MAP/MLE starting point; poor initial guesses for $\beta$ are the usual failure mode since the exponential is sensitive, so seed $\alpha,\gamma$ from the observed frequency range and $\beta$ from the log-slope of the trace. Then implement an MCMC sampler (Metropolis-Hastings, or `emcee`) around that starting point, launching at least 2 chains in true parallel processes (e.g. `multiprocessing.Pool` or `joblib`, not just sequential loops labeled as chains), discard a burn-in prefix from each chain, concatenate the remainder, and take the posterior mean per parameter. Write exactly `{"alpha":..., "beta":..., "gamma":...}` to `/home/user/estimates.json`, and sanity-check the fitted curve against the raw spectrogram trace before finalizing.
