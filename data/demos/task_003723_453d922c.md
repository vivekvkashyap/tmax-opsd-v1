For the C loader: compute SHA-256 of the file's full contents via OpenSSL's `SHA256()` (or the EVP API) and format it as lowercase hex to compare against the known-good hash string; on mismatch, write to stderr and `exit(1)`. On match, read the entire secret into a buffer *before* sandboxing, then call `prctl(PR_SET_SECCOMP, SECCOMP_MODE_STRICT)` — after that call only `read`/`write`/`_exit`/`sigreturn` are permitted, so do all hashing, comparison, and buffering beforehand and only `write()` the buffered secret to stdout afterward; any other syscall (e.g. libc buffered I/O that might call something else, or using `printf`) after entering strict mode will kill the process. Compile with `-lcrypto` and pipe stdout to `verified_secret.txt`. Pitfall: calling exit() instead of `_exit()` after sandboxing can invoke disallowed syscalls via atexit handlers/stdio flushing.
