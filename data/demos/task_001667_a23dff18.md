Treat this as two separable subproblems: (1) a statistics computation and (2) a supervised threshold search. For the baseline, compute per-frame spectral flatness (geometric mean of the magnitude spectrum divided by its arithmetic mean, per STFT frame) across the whole ambient file, then bootstrap: resample the frame-level flatness values with replacement 5000 times, compute the mean of each resample, and take the 2.5th/97.5th percentiles of that distribution of means as your CI — do not just take percentiles of the raw per-frame values, the CI must be over the *mean statistic*. For the classifier, compute the STFT magnitude matrix with the exact NFFT=1024/hop=512/Hann-window params specified, take its condition number (e.g. via `numpy.linalg.cond`), then search over a grid of candidate thresholds using the labeled clean/evil training examples to find one value that cleanly separates the two groups' condition numbers (evil audio like silence, DC, or pure tones is rank-deficient/near-singular and has a much higher condition number than well-conditioned noisy/harmonic clean audio) — hardcode that threshold.
