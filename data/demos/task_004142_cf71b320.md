Build this as two independent Bash artifacts: an offline trainer and a stateless TCP server, both driven only by coreutils/awk/bc (no Python). For training (`/home/user/train.sh`): install socat/netcat, then for each line in `/home/user/dataset/logs.txt` call `/app/log_oracle "$line"` to get its MALICIOUS/BENIGN label — treat the oracle purely as a labeling function, don't try to guess its internal rule. From the labeled lines compute: class priors (fraction of lines in each class), and per-word likelihoods P(word|class) with Laplace (add-1) smoothing over the full vocabulary observed in the dataset (denominator = word count in that class + vocab size). Persist priors and per-word/class counts to `/home/user/model.txt` in a simple parseable format (e.g. `word class count`). For serving (`/home/user/serve.sh`): use `socat TCP-LISTEN:9090,reuseaddr,fork EXEC:.../handler.sh` (or a netcat loop) to accept lines of the form `AUTH:<token>|LOG:<snippet>`; parse with `awk`/parameter expansion, reject non-matching tokens with the exact literal `ERROR: UNAUTHORIZED`, otherwise tokenize the snippet, look up each word's smoothed likelihoods in `model.txt`, multiply through priors in log-space with `awk`/`bc` for numerical stability, convert back to a normalized probability, and print `RESULT:<value>` rounded to 4 decimals. Launch `serve.sh` backgrounded (e.g. `nohup ... &disown`) and verify it's still listening with `ss -tlnp` before finishing.
