This is a classic AES-CBC bit-flipping attack — exploit the fact that flipping bits in one ciphertext block's IV/preceding-ciphertext-block flips the same bits in the corresponding position of the next decrypted plaintext block, without needing the key. Craft a plaintext of the exact same length as your target payload (`<svg onload=1>`, 14 characters) but replacing the forbidden `<` and `>` characters with two other printable ASCII characters (e.g. 'X' and 'Y') at the same positions, so it passes the WAF filter when POSTed to `/encrypt`. Take the returned IV and ciphertext, then XOR the corresponding IV byte at the position of your substitute character with `(substitute_char XOR target_char)` — do this for both substituted positions — leaving the ciphertext untouched. Submit the modified `iv:ciphertext` token to `/submit`; since CBC decryption of the first block XORs the IV directly into the plaintext, this flips exactly your two substitute characters back into `<` and `>`, producing the exact target string despite the WAF never seeing it.
