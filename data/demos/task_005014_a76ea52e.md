First load the reference WAV with `soundfile`, take the real FFT (`numpy.fft.rfft`), get magnitudes, and normalize to sum to 1 — this is your distribution P. Threshold at 0.05 to print `CLEAN` or `EVIL`. Watch two common mistakes: (a) forgetting to sort/select the peak values of P rather than using raw FFT bin order, which breaks distributional comparison; (b) not truncating P to exactly `min(N,M)` entries before comparing against Q, causing a shape/support mismatch.
