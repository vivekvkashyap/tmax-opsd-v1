Build and validate the signal-processing core before wrapping it in a server. Extract frames from `/app/flicker_experiment.mp4` (e.g. with OpenCV `cv2.VideoCapture` or `ffmpeg` piped to raw frames), convert each to grayscale, and compute the mean pixel intensity per frame to form a 1D time series sampled at the known 30 FPS. Apply an FFT (e.g. `numpy.fft.rfft`) to a given slice of this series, convert bin indices to Hz using the 30 FPS sample rate and the slice length, and take the frequency (excluding the 0 Hz/DC bin) with the largest magnitude as the dominant frequency. For significance, compare that peak's amplitude against `3.0 *` the mean amplitude of all other non-zero-frequency bins in the same slice — recompute this per-request on the exact requested `[start_frame, end_frame]` slice, not on the whole signal, since short/noisy slices are expected to come back non-significant. Then wrap this logic in an HTTP server (any language) listening on `127.0.0.1:8080`, exposing `POST /analyze` that parses the JSON body, slices the precomputed time series, reruns the FFT+significance check on just that slice, and returns `{"dominant_frequency_hz": <rounded float>, "is_significant": <bool>}`, with basic error handling for out-of-range or too-short slices. Keep the server running (foreground or background) for testing.
