Build this as a self-contained optimization pipeline. Set up the venv and install numpy/scipy/biopython first, and use Biopython's SeqIO to parse the FASTA file rather than hand-rolling a parser. In your objective function: (1) build the numerical signal by mapping each base to its weight, (2) explicitly renormalize the weight vector to unit L2 norm INSIDE the objective (not just once at the start) since scipy.optimize.minimize will move the weights away from unit norm during the search — this is the step naive attempts skip, causing the optimizer to just blow up |w| to trivially inflate power instead of genuinely maximizing spectral concentration. Compute the FFT via `numpy.fft.fft`, take the squared magnitude for the power spectrum, and compute the ratio of power at bin `k = N/3` (integer index, exact division since N is a multiple of 3) to the total power summed over all N bins. Minimize the negative of that ratio with `scipy.optimize.minimize` (Nelder-Mead) from the given initial guess. After convergence, apply the final normalized weights to every sequence in the FASTA file, compute each one's R value, and sort descending to build the ranking list before writing the JSON output.
