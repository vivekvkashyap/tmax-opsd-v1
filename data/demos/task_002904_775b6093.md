Do this in three clearly separated passes rather than trying to combine them in one clever one-liner. First, bucket: compute `bucket = (timestamp // 300) * 300` for every row. Second, aggregate: group by `(sensor_id, bucket)` and take the max temperature within each group — this is your "real" per-bucket reading. Third, impute: for each sensor independently, determine its min and max bucket timestamp, generate the *complete* sequence of 5-minute buckets between them (step 300), and for any bucket missing from your aggregated data, carry forward the value from the nearest preceding bucket (not the raw previous row) — a clean way is to build a dict of known bucket->temp per sensor, then walk the full bucket sequence in order keeping a "last seen" variable to fill gaps. Finally sort by `sensor_id` then `bucket_timestamp` and write the CSV with header `bucket_timestamp,sensor_id,temperature`, formatting temperature with exactly one decimal place (`f"{t:.1f}"`). Pandas (`groupby().max()`, `reindex` per group) makes the fill-forward step much less error-prone than manual awk logic.
