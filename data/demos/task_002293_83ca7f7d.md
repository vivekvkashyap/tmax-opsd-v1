Process each backup directory independently in a loop over `/home/user/legacy_archive/*/`. For each: (1) reassemble chunks in alphabetical order with `cat chunk_* > full.bin` (globbing already sorts alphabetically, but confirm), then split off the header with `dd if=full.bin bs=1 count=8 of=header.bin` (or `head -c 8`) and the payload with `dd if=full.bin bs=1 skip=8 of=payload.gz` (or `tail -c +9`) — off-by-one here is the most common mistake, since byte 9 onward means skipping exactly 8 bytes. (2) Decompress the payload with `gunzip -c payload.gz > data.txt`. (3) Compute the header's lowercase hex with `xxd -p header.bin | tr -d '\n'` and append a line `VERIFIED: <hex>` to `data.txt`. (4) Recompress with `bzip2 -c data.txt > data.bz2`, then `split -b 51200 -d -a 2 data.bz2` renamed/prefixed to produce `part_aa`, `part_ab`, ... (use `split`'s alphabetic suffix mode, not numeric, to match the naming). (5) Write header and split chunks into a fresh `/tmp/staging_<name>/` directory (both `header.bin` at the new_archive level and a `chunks/` subdir), then only `mv` the fully-populated staging directory into `/home/user/new_archive/<name>/` as the final atomic step — never write partial output directly into the destination path.
