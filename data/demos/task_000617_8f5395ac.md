Treat this as a graph-construction-then-filter pipeline, not a one-shot query. Load the edge list with `networkx.read_edgelist` (or manually parse comma-separated rows) into an undirected `Graph`, then compute `nx.degree_centrality(G)` once and reuse it — recomputing per-node degree by hand instead of using centrality (which normalizes by `n-1`) is the most common naive mistake and will silently break the >0.10 threshold check. Classify nodes by their prefix (`A:`, `M:`, `D:`) using string checks. First find actors connected to any movie that is itself connected to any director (a 2-hop pattern match through the movie node, not a direct actor-director edge). Then restrict directors to "top directors" — those with centrality strictly greater than 0.10 — and keep only actors whose qualifying movie is directed by one of those top directors. Among the surviving actors, pick the one with highest degree centrality, breaking ties alphabetically by node ID. Write only that single ID (no newline assumptions beyond exact match) to the output file, and sanity-check your intermediate sets by printing them during development before trusting the final answer.
