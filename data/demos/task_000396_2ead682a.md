Build this as a pipeline, not a one-shot script. First, use OpenCV to extract the red bob's (x,y) pixel centroid per frame: threshold on the red channel (e.g. `cv2.inRange` for high-R/low-G/low-B), find contours, take the centroid of the largest one. Convert each (x,y) to an angle via `theta = atan2(x - pivot_x, y - pivot_y)` (angle from vertical, since the pendulum hangs down from the pivot at top-center) — verify sign/offset conventions against a couple of frames by eye. Convert pixel length to meters using the given pixels-per-meter scale to get the pendulum's effective length in pixels vs. physical estimate, but treat L as a free parameter to fit rather than assuming it from the rod length directly. Fit b and L by minimizing squared error between simulated theta(t) (via `scipy.integrate.solve_ivp` on the nonlinear ODE) and your extracted theta(t) trajectory, using `scipy.optimize.minimize` or `curve_fit` with reasonable bounds/initial guesses. Derive initial conditions (theta0, theta_dot0) from the first few extracted frames via finite differences, not by assuming zero velocity. Finally integrate with an adaptive-step solver (RK45, tight rtol/atol) at exactly the 30 FPS grid points from t=0 to just under t=10, and write time/theta/theta_dot to CSV with correct column names. The main pitfalls: getting the angle sign/reference convention wrong, and not fitting L/b tightly enough (loose optimizer tolerances leave residual error above threshold).
